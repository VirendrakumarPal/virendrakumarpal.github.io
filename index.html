Convictions

Four convictions, each traced to a system shipped.

01.

Decision economics outranks model accuracy.

A four-point AUC improvement can underperform the previous model in P&L. The marginal customers gained may already be the ones being correctly declined. Every system review must start with the business metric — and use statistical output as a diagnostic, not a goal.

Alt-data underwriting at MSME lending was designed around credit access for the underserved. Business outcome first; model performance as the measure of delivery. The four-year head start became the moat — not the model.

02.

Architecture is the differentiator. Models are commodity.

Most enterprises can rent the same models. They cannot rent the architecture that turns 20+ specialist agents into a coherent decision lifecycle. The defensible work is at the orchestration layer. The model is the engine; the architecture is the car.

20–25 production AI/ML models orchestrated across the credit lifecycle at LenDenClub — underwriting, matching, collections — producing decisions in seconds, repayment forecasts at 95–96% accuracy.

03.

The organisation is the product. The models are the output.

The true test of AI leadership is what the team continues to ship — and how they decide — after the leader leaves. Culture, decision frameworks and talent pipelines are the real deliverables. Models depreciate. Functions compound.

Two AI organisations built from zero (SMEcorner 2017, LenDenClub 2022) continued to operate and scale after each leadership transition. The org outlasted the build phase. That is the point.

04.

Categories are shipped before they are named.

AutoML 2013, document AI 2017, alt-data lending 2018, voice/meeting AI 2019, multimodal AI 2020, agentic AI 2024. Each was shipped while the rest of the market was still naming it. That is not luck — it is a read on what model architecture, the buyer, and the operating environment have all just agreed on, before consensus crystallises.

The pattern arc above is the proof. Six categories. Twelve years. One operator. Each preceded a public market category by 12–36 months.
