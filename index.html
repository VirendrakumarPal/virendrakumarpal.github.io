Operating Philosophy

Four principles.

Each one traces back to specific systems shipped between 2011 and 2024.

01.

Multi-agent AI is a governance choice before it is a technology choice.

The reason to go multi-agent in regulated finance is auditability. When each step is a separate agent, every hand-off emits a reason code. The risk committee can walk the graph. The regulator can walk the graph. Build the graph as if it were the audit trail — because it is.

02.

Decision economics outranks model accuracy.

A four-point AUC lift can underperform the older model in P&L. The marginal customers may be the ones already being rejected. Every model review starts with the business metric — rupees per disbursed loan, NPA at month six, approval rate at fixed risk ceiling — and uses statistical metrics as diagnostics, not goals.

03.

Governance is not a tax on velocity. It is the only reason anyone trusts you enough to scale.

Five documents before any Gen AI system ships: intended use, data provenance, evaluation plan, risk register, rollback plan. Three engineering days to write. The cost of not writing them is one regulatory letter or one bad headline. SR 11-7 has asked for most of this since 2011.

04.

Build the people who build the AI.

The true test of leadership is what your team becomes without you. Empower people to think, not depend on you for answers. Shape a culture that fixes problems, not blame. Leadership is not about answers — it is about the mindset you pass on.
